Muḥammad bin Mūsā al-Khawārizmī

Muḥammad bin Mūsā al-Khawārizmī (Persian: موسى الخوارزمي) is an expert in mathematics, astronomy, astrology, and geography from Kufa, Iraq. Born around 780 in Khwārizm (present-day Khiva, Uzbekistan) and died around 850 in Baghdad. For most of his life, he worked as a lecturer at the Honorary School in Baghdad founded by the Abbasid Caliph Al-Ma'mun bin Ar-Rashid, where he studied natural sciences and mathematics, including studying translations of Sanskrit and Greek manuscripts

The following is a list of books by Alkhwarizmi :

  • Book I: Algebra,

    Al-Kitāb al-mukhtaṣar fī isāb al-jabr wa-l-muqābala (Arabic: الكتاب المختصر اب الجبر والمقابلة or The Book of Complementing and Balancing Calculations) is a mathematics book written in 830. This book summarizes the definition of algebra.

  • Book 2: Dixit algorithm,

    Another book of al-Khawārizmī is on arithmetic

  • Book 3: Planetarium Reconstruction,

    His third famous book is the Kitāb rat al-Arḍ (Arabic: اب الأرض "Book of Sights of the World" or "Appearances of the Earth" translated by Geography), which was completed in 833 is a revision and refinement of Ptolemy's Geography, consisting of a list of 2402 coordinates of cities and other geographical places follow the general development.

  • Book 4: Astronomy,

    The book Zīj al-sindhind (Arabic: "astronomical tables") is a work consisting of 37 symbols on astronomical calendar calculations and 116 tables with calendar, astronomical and astrological data as well as currently recognized data.

  • Book 5: The Jewish Calendar,

    Al-Khawārizmī also wrote about the Jewish Calendar (Risāla fi istikhrāj taʾrīkh al-yahd "Guidelines for the Jewish Calendar"). Which explains the 19 year cycle of intercalation, the law governing what day of the week the month of Tishr begins.

  • Otehr Books,

    Several Arabic manuscripts in Berlin, Istanbul, Tashkent, Cairo and Paris contain a material approach that probably comes from al-Khwarizmī. Manuscripts in Istanbul contain sundials, which are mentioned in the Fihirst. Other works, such as the determination of the direction of Mecca is one of spherical astronomy. Two works contain about morning (Ma'rifat sa'at al-mashriq fī kull balad) and determination of azimuth from height (Ma'rifat al-samt min qibal al-irtifā'). He also wrote 2 books on the use and assembly of the astrolabe. Ibn al-Nadim in his Kitab al-Fihrist (an index of Arabic) also mentions the Kitāb ar-Ruḵāma(t) (the sundial book) and the Kitab al-Tarikh (the history book) but the latter two are said to have been lost.

"Khwarizmi's text is so different, not only from the Babylonian books, but also from Diophantus' Arithmatics. It is no longer a matter of a number of problems to solve, but a show that begins with simple terms whose combination gives all the possibilities for basic equations, which from now on is explicitly the correct object of study"
